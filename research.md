## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **ISO 41001** — International standard for facility management systems; defines requirements for effective FM governance and continuous improvement
- **IFMA (International Facility Management Association) Standards** — Competency framework and best-practice guidance for FM professionals, widely referenced in platform feature design
- **ISO 55000** — Asset management standard governing the lifecycle management of physical assets, directly applicable to CMMS and IWMS platforms
- **BOMA Standards** — Building Owners and Managers Association measurement and reporting standards for commercial real estate space; used in space-planning modules
- **ENERGY STAR / ISO 50001** — Energy management standards informing sustainability dashboards and reporting in FM platforms
- **COBie (Construction Operations Building Information Exchange)** — Data schema for transferring built-asset information from construction into FM systems

## Market Research

**Market Size:** The global FM software market is estimated at USD 26.3 billion in 2025, projected to reach USD 81.8 billion by 2035 at a 12% CAGR. The US market alone is projected to grow from USD 14.9 billion in 2025 to USD 30.6 billion by 2030, driven by digital buildings and ESG mandates.

**Funding:** IBM, Oracle, and SAP dominate through suite sales. Facilio raised a Series B in 2023. UpKeep attracted Series B funding (~$50M). The mid-market CMMS segment continues to attract SaaS-native investment.

**Pricing Landscape:** Enterprise IWMS platforms (Archibus, Planon, TRIRIGA) typically cost £30,000–£150,000+ per year with multi-year implementation cycles. CMMS-focused tools (UpKeep, Fiix) start at $45/user/month. Maximo starts at ~$164/user/month.

**Key Buyer Personas:** Corporate real estate and facilities directors managing large office portfolios; healthcare and campus operators needing compliance-grade maintenance tracking; property managers optimising multi-site space utilisation; sustainability officers reporting on energy and carbon metrics.

**Notable Trends:** IoT integration has grown 34% year-on-year, with over 15 billion square feet of commercial real estate now digitally managed. The shift from reactive to predictive maintenance is the dominant operational trend. Hybrid work patterns have driven demand for occupancy sensing and desk/room booking integrated into FM platforms. ESG reporting requirements are accelerating energy management module adoption.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- Predictive maintenance that fuses IoT sensor streams, historical work-order data, and equipment age to predict failures days or weeks before they occur, reducing unplanned downtime
- AI-driven space optimisation that analyses badge, calendar, and sensor data to recommend space reconfigurations, reducing real estate costs without sacrificing employee experience
- Automated work-order triage and routing that reads natural-language fault reports, classifies priority, assigns the nearest qualified technician, and drafts the work instruction
- Conversational FM assistant for occupants to report issues, book spaces, and get building-service status in natural language via web, mobile, or Teams/Slack
- Energy anomaly detection that identifies consumption outliers across a building portfolio in real time and generates root-cause hypotheses for facilities engineers
